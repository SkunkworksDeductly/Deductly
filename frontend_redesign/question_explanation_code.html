Question 14 of 25 — Section 2

Time spent: 1:42
Stimulus

"The recent decline in honeybee populations is often attributed to pesticide use. However, some researchers point to habitat loss as the primary driver. If it were solely pesticides, we would see uniform declines across all agricultural areas, yet many regions with high pesticide use have stable populations."

Which of the following most accurately describes the role played by the claim that uniform declines would be seen if pesticides were the sole cause?

B

It is the conclusion the argument seeks to establish.

Your Choice — Incorrect
close
D

It is a premise offered in support of the argument's main conclusion.

Correct Answer
check

The Breakdown

The argument aims to challenge the "solely pesticides" hypothesis. To do this, it introduces a conditional claim: If X (pesticides) were the sole cause, then Y (uniform decline) would occur.

By showing that Y (uniform decline) is not actually occurring, the author concludes that the antecedent (X) must not be the sole cause. Therefore, this statement serves as a premise—a logical building block used to disprove the competing theory.

lightbulb

Key Concept: Modus Tollens

Logic Pattern

If P, then Q. Not Q.
Therefore, Not P.

In this question

This valid deductive form is the skeleton of the stimulus. Identifying the "skeleton" prevents distraction by the "subject matter."

Common Pitfall

"Many students confuse the supporting conditional premise for the conclusion itself because it carries the 'logical weight' of the argument's movement."
